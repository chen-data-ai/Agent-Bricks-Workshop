# Internet Connectivity Service Policy

## 1. Overview

This policy document outlines the standards, procedures, and responsibilities related to internet connectivity services provided by our company. It serves as a reference for both customers and employees regarding how internet connectivity issues are addressed, prevented, and resolved.

## 2. Service Standards

### 2.1 Connectivity Guarantees
- Standard residential service: 99.5% uptime measured on a monthly basis
- Business service: 99.9% uptime with Service Level Agreements (SLAs)
- Enterprise service: 99.99% uptime with enhanced SLAs and priority restoration

### 2.2 Speed and Performance
- Customers will receive at minimum 80% of their subscribed speed during non-peak hours
- During peak hours (7:00 PM - 11:00 PM local time), minimum guaranteed speeds may reduce to 70% of subscribed rates
- Latency targets: <30ms for fiber, <50ms for cable, <100ms for DSL to first hop

## 3. Scheduled Maintenance

### 3.1 Maintenance Windows
- Routine maintenance: Tuesdays and Thursdays, 2:00 AM - 5:00 AM local time
- Extended maintenance: First Sunday of each month, 12:00 AM - 6:00 AM local time
- Emergency maintenance: May occur outside scheduled windows when necessary to protect network integrity

### 3.2 Impact Minimization
- Maintenance activities will be designed to minimize service disruption
- Where possible, redundant systems will maintain connectivity during maintenance
- Business and Enterprise customers may request custom maintenance schedules

## 4. Customer Notification Protocols

### 4.1 Scheduled Maintenance Notifications
- Standard notification: 7 days advance notice via email and account portal
- Emergency maintenance: Minimum 24-hour notice when possible
- Notification methods: Email, SMS (opt-in), in-app notifications, account portal alerts

### 4.2 Outage Communications
- Initial notification within 30 minutes of confirmed widespread outage
- Status updates every 60 minutes until resolution
- Post-incident report available within 48 hours for Business and Enterprise customers
- Service status available via:
  - Company website status page
  - Mobile app (iOS and Android)
  - Customer service phone line

## 5. Troubleshooting and Remediation Procedures

### 5.1 First-Level Support
- 24/7 technical support via phone, chat, and mobile app
- Remote diagnostics of customer equipment when permitted
- Guided self-service troubleshooting via online portal and mobile app

### 5.2 Escalation Process
- Tier 1: Basic troubleshooting and known issue identification
- Tier 2: Advanced troubleshooting and configuration support
- Tier 3: Network engineering and specialized technical support
- Field Service: On-site technical support when remote resolution is not possible

### 5.3 Resolution Timeframes
- Residential service: Best effort resolution within 24-48 hours
- Business service: Resolution within 8-24 hours per SLA
- Enterprise service: Resolution within 4-8 hours per SLA

## 6. Customer Responsibilities

### 6.1 Equipment and Environment
- Maintain company-provided equipment in appropriate environmental conditions
- Follow recommended setup guidelines for customer-owned equipment
- Provide adequate power, ventilation, and protection from water/physical damage
- Report any physical damage to equipment promptly

### 6.2 Access and Cooperation
- Provide reasonable access to premises for installation and repair
- Follow troubleshooting instructions when reporting issues
- Maintain current contact information for service notifications
- Promptly respond to requests for information during troubleshooting

### 6.3 Acceptable Use
- Adhere to the Acceptable Use Policy regarding bandwidth consumption
- Refrain from activities that may disrupt network performance
- Secure your network to prevent unauthorized access and malicious activity

## 7. Company Obligations

### 7.1 Service Restoration
- Prioritize restoration of services based on outage scope and customer tier
- Deploy appropriate resources to resolve connectivity issues
- Provide loaner equipment when necessary during extended repair periods

### 7.2 Service Credits
- Automatic service credits applied when uptime guarantees are not met
- Additional credits available upon request for extended outages
